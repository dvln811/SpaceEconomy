Economy Design

The economy is a living production chain. Raw materials are mined, refined into materials, manufactured into components, and assembled into final products. Every item flows through this chain via NPC and player traders.

Production Chain

Flow: Asteroid Fields → Mining Colony → Refinery → Industrial Hub → Component Factory → Shipyard

Volume Design: Common ores are tiny (0.1 m3) so ships can haul thousands per trip. Exotic materials are bulky (1-2 m3) limiting supply. Final products are 2-5 m3 each.

Economic Mechanics

Pricing: Supply/demand driven. Stations track pressure over time. Unfilled demand raises buy price. Oversupply lowers sell price. Prices update every 10 ticks.

Self-Limiting Production: Stations produce at a rate limited by available inputs. If supply drops, production smoothly slows rather than halting abruptly. As logistics improve, production automatically scales up.

Trade AI: NPC traders see all buy/sell opportunities across their entire cluster. They pick the most profitable route and haul goods accordingly. Dynamic pricing ensures resources flow where they're needed.

Mining: Mining colonies with asteroid fields passively generate ore. NPC miners also extract from belts and sell at local stations. Higher density fields produce faster.
