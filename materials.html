T2-T3: Refined & Manufactured Materials

Raw ores are processed into refined materials at refineries (T2), then combined into advanced manufactured materials at industrial hubs (T3). Each step adds value and reduces volume.

Design Notes

Recipe Logic:
- Steel needs iron + carbon (logical metallurgy)
- Titanium alloy needs titanium + chromium (real aerospace practice)
- Electronics need silicon + copper + gold (mirrors real manufacturing)
- Fuel needs ice (hydrogen extraction) or helium-3 (fusion)
- Exotic materials (Quantum Filament) require null-sec inputs

Volume Compression: Refining reduces volume significantly (10 m3 ore -> 4 m3 refined -> 2 m3 alloy). This means higher-tier goods are easier to haul but have much more value per m3.

Station Tier Mapping:
- Refineries: T1 -> T2 (simple processing)
- Industrial Hubs: T2 -> T3 (complex manufacturing)
- Component Factories: T3 -> T4 (assembly, next page)
- Shipyards: T4 -> T5 (final products, next page)
