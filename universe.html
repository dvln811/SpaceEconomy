Universe Structure

The accessible sector contains 48 star systems organized into 3 clusters connected by jump nexus chokepoints. Each cluster has a distinct security profile and economic character. The universe is generated once and persists permanently, evolving slowly through economic activity.

The universe is not procedurally regenerated. It is a fixed world that grows and changes through player and NPC activity. Think of it as a living place, not a random seed.

Chokepoints

  • Procyon (Core): Nexus connecting core to rim via Castor/Arcturus. Component factory here.
  • Castor (Rim): Major junction between rim and frontier. Gateway to Wolf 359, Rigel.
  • Spica (Frontier): Deep frontier nexus connecting Terminus, Obsidian, The Abyss.

Ore Distribution Rules

The rarer the ore, the further you have to go to find it, and the more dangerous the trip to sell it.
Ore CategoryExamplesFound InDistance to Buyer
CommonIron Ore, Copper Ore, Ice, OrganicsCore + Rim0 jumps (local refineries OK)
ModerateTitanium Ore, Helium-3Rim1+ jumps to processor
RarePlatinum, Crystals, Rare Earths, UraniumFrontier/Null-sec only2+ jumps to any buyer

This creates natural trade routes: miners must haul rare ores through dangerous space to reach processors in safer systems. Larger, braver ships take the direct route. Smaller ships must find longer, safer paths.

Navigation Model

Two-Layer Travel

  • Inter-system: Ships travel between star systems via jump gates. ~50 seconds per hop at speed 1.0.
  • Intra-system: Ships travel between objects within a system (stations, belts, gates). ~35-40 seconds per trip at 0.2 AU/tick.
  • Jump gates: One per connection, placed at the outer ring (10-13 AU from star). Must intra-travel to gate before jumping.

System Objects

Each system contains: a central star, 2-4 planets (with moons), stations (mid ring 3-7 AU), asteroid belts (4-9 AU), jump gates (outer ring 10-13 AU). All positioned with polar coordinates (distance from star + angle).

NPC Fleet

70 NPC ships operate autonomously, making trade and mining decisions each tick. Ship class determines risk tolerance, which controls what systems the ship is willing to enter.
