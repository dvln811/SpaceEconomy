Mining Mechanic

Mining is the safety net. You can always mine. It's slow money but guaranteed money. No one goes broke if they're willing to work a rock.

How Mining Works

Asteroid fields exist in most systems. You fly to the field, target rocks, and extract ore over time. Mining is a real-time activity that produces commodities directly into your cargo hold.

Mining Loop
  1. Travel to asteroid field - costs time and fuel, like any destination
  2. Scan rocks - scanner hardpoint reveals composition and yield
  3. Mine - mining laser extracts ore over time into cargo hold
  4. Haul back to station - sell raw ore, or haul to a refinery for better prices

Mining Properties

  • Always available - asteroid fields don't deplete (they regenerate slowly). You can always make money mining.
  • Scales with gear - better mining laser = faster extraction. Better scanner = find richer rocks. Bigger hold = fewer trips.
  • Risk/reward by location - high-sec belts have common ore (low value). Null-sec belts have rare/exotic materials (high value, danger).
  • Affected by economy - ore prices fluctuate with demand. A shipyard boom means alloy-grade ore prices spike.
  • NPC miners compete - NPC mining ships also work the belts, contributing to supply.
  • Not passive - you choose which rocks, manage cargo space, decide when to haul back vs keep mining.

Mining Economy Integration

  • Raw ore sells at mining colonies and refineries (base price, always a buyer)
  • Refined materials sell at industrial hubs and shipyards (higher price, requires refinery step)
  • Supply chain: Mine ore > Sell to refinery > Refinery produces alloys > Shipyard buys alloys
  • Player choice: Sell raw (quick, low margin) or haul to refinery system (more time, better price)
  • Market impact: Heavy mining in one field increases local supply, drops local price. Miners naturally spread out.
