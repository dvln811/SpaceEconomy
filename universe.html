Universe Structure

The galaxy contains ~2,500 star systems organized into regions, constellations, and clusters. Most of the galaxy starts unclaimed and wild. Factions hold small core territories and expand outward over months and years of real-time simulation. Security levels evolve as factions build infrastructure and project force.

The universe is persistent and evolves through NPC faction activity. Systems that are wild today may be civilized in six months. Systems that are safe today may fall to warfare. Think of it as a living place with a history that writes itself.

Security Level Progression

Security is not a fixed property of a system. It evolves based on faction activity:

How Security Rises

  • Station construction - building a station begins civilizing a system
  • Military patrols - faction fleets patrolling raises security
  • Trade volume - sustained commerce indicates stability
  • Time - security rises slowly even with minimal activity (weeks per level)

How Security Falls

  • Station destruction - warfare destroying infrastructure drops security
  • Pirate activity - Corsair raids degrade security
  • Abandonment - systems with no faction presence slowly regress
  • Blockades - cutting off supply lines starves stations

Progression Timeline (per system)

TransitionRequirementsReal-Time
Wild -> LowFirst station built, basic patrols~1 week
Low -> Medium2+ stations, regular trade, military presence~2-3 weeks
Medium -> HighFull infrastructure, sustained peace, high trade volume~4-6 weeks

At ~1 system per week average advancement, civilizing the entire galaxy would take roughly 3-5 years of continuous simulation (with setbacks from warfare slowing it further).

Faction Expansion Mechanics

How Factions Grow

  • Factions target adjacent unclaimed systems for expansion
  • They build stations (requires hauling construction materials)
  • They assign patrol fleets to protect new territory
  • They establish mining operations for local resources
  • Expansion rate limited by economy (can't build stations without materials)

Expansion Priorities (AI)

  • Resource-rich systems - rare ore deposits attract colonization
  • Strategic chokepoints - control jump nexus systems for trade control
  • Buffer zones - expand toward enemies to create defensive depth
  • Trade routes - connect isolated colonies to the main economy

Conflict Over Territory

  • Two factions expanding toward the same system creates a flashpoint
  • Warfare breaks out over contested systems
  • Winner claims the system, loser's stations are destroyed or captured
  • Border systems are perpetually contested (low-sec buffer zones)

Universe Generation (Planned)

2,500 systems will be procedurally generated with constraints:

Generation Rules

  • Faction homeworlds hand-placed at specific coordinates with pre-built infrastructure
  • Jump gate network connects systems (average 3-4 connections per system, some chokepoints with 1-2)
  • Ore distribution follows security gradient - common ores everywhere, rare ores only in deep null-sec
  • System types distributed logically - mining systems near ore, shipyards near faction capitals
  • Distance from faction center = danger - the further out, the less infrastructure, the more wild

System Contents

Each system contains:

  • A central star (type varies: main sequence, red giant, binary, etc.)
  • 2-6 planets with moons
  • 0-4 asteroid belts (more in mining-rich systems)
  • 0-3 stations (in claimed systems, more in developed ones)
  • Jump gates (one per connection, at outer ring)

Navigation Model

Two-Layer Travel

  • Inter-system: Jump gates connect systems. Travel time varies by distance (30-120 seconds per hop).
  • Intra-system: Travel between objects within a system (stations, belts, gates). 30-90 seconds depending on distance and ship speed.
  • Align time: Ships take 2-15 seconds to align before warping intra-system (larger ships = slower).

Scale Implications

With 2,500 systems, crossing the galaxy takes many jumps. This means:

  • Local economies are somewhat isolated (prices vary by region)
  • Hauling rare ore from null-sec to core is a significant logistics operation
  • Factions cannot instantly project force across their territory
  • Chokepoint systems have enormous strategic value

NPC Ship Simulation

At 2,500 systems, the NPC population scales to 100,000+ ships. These are simulated abstractly:

Performance Model

  • Each NPC is a state machine: {system, destination, state, cargo, faction, ship_class}
  • Ships only need detailed simulation when player is in the same system
  • Aggregate behavior (trade volume, mining output) can be calculated per-system without individual tick processing
  • Military fleets simulated as unit groups, not individual ships
  • Target: simulate 100K+ ships at 1 tick/sec on shared-cpu-2x
