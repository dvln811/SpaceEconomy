# Economy Tuning Log - June 27, 2026

## Overview
5 rounds of simulate -> analyze -> tweak to achieve a stable population-driven economy.
Each round: 10K ticks (~42 game-days) at 8-16 t/s.

## Round 0 (Pre-tuning baseline)
- Flat 2.0/tick consumption per station (no population scaling)
- 507 haulers, old assigned-station logic
- Prices: wildly unstable, 0.054x to 18x range
- Growth: massive accumulation

## Round 1: Population Scaling + Fuel Fix
**Changes:**
- Consumption: rate = 2.0 * max(0.5, pop/100M) per commodity
- Removed fuel consumption from refineries/factories/component_works
- Military/shipyard self-supply at 1.5x consumption rate (cap 500)
- Passive gen reduced 20 -> 8
- Contract-based hauler AI (replaces assigned-station logic)
- Hauler fleet increased 507 -> ~1600
- Industrial cargo bumped (Clydesdale 100K, Mammoth 30K, Ox 15K)

**Results:**
- Fuel: avg 1.10x (FIXED - was 1.97x)
- Military bases: stocked (4500 ammo, 9500 wpn, 1000 fuel)
- Hauler utilization: 72% (was 18%)
- Ore prices: 1.4-1.6x avg (still elevated)
- Growth: 9,300/tick

## Round 2: Refinery Boost
**Changes:**
- Refinery production_rate: 1.0 -> 3.0 (3x throughput)
- Military self-supply cap: 5000 -> 500

**Results:**
- Fuel: avg 1.09x (stable)
- Ore: Iron 1.46x, Copper 1.60x (slight improvement)
- Growth: 10,126/tick (slight increase from faster refinery output)
- Military: properly capped at 4500/9500/1000

## Round 3: Mining Yield Increase
**Changes:**
- Mining yield: 200 -> 400 per cycle
- Passive gen: 8 -> 3 (reduce free stockpiling)

**Results:**
- Nearly identical to Round 2 (ore still 1.4-1.6x)
- Confirms bottleneck is LOGISTICS, not production
- Haulers at 72% utilization with 5.7M units in transit
- Growth: 9,300/tick (decelerating from R2)

## Final Assessment

### What's Working:
- Population-driven consumption creates demand hotspots (high-sec > null-sec)
- Fuel economy solved (only trade hubs/military/shipyards consume externally)
- Military bases self-sustain with capped stockpiles
- Contract-based hauler AI achieves 72% utilization
- Production chains functional (ore -> refined -> manufactured -> components)
- Hard price clamp (0.5x-2.0x) prevents runaway inflation/deflation
- 7M+ units actively in transit at any time

### Remaining Issues:
- Growth rate 9,300/tick (ore stockpiling at mining colonies)
  - Not critical: stockpiles represent available supply for haulers
  - Will self-limit when inventory_cap (500K) is reached
- 56% of prices at 2.0x ceiling
  - This is geographic friction (logistics can't service all demand points)
  - Will improve with: more haulers, combat attrition sinking goods, faction events
  - The hard clamp makes this a visual issue, not an economic failure
- Median price 2.0x
  - Need to consider relaxing the clamp to 0.5x-3.0x for more natural variation
  - OR accept that the hard clamp is an artifical stability mechanism

### Next Steps:
1. Wire combat attrition into the sim (ships destroyed = real demand sink)
2. Consider relaxing price clamp once combat provides natural demand
3. Add more haulers as fleet grows organically
4. Test with event injection (sim_events.py) for resilience

## Economy Worker Changes:
- Consumption: `rate = 2.0 * max(0.5, sys.population / 100_000_000.0)`
- Price clamp: `max(bp * 0.5, min(bp * 2.0, new_price))`
- Pressure: +/-0.2 per update, cap +/-15, decay 0.90
- Military self-supply: `2.0 * pop_mult * 1.5` when inventory < 500
- Fuel removed from refinery/factory/component_works consumption

## NPC Hauler Changes:
- Contract-based system (replaces assigned-station)
- Contracts rebuild every 50 ticks from all station deficits
- Inter-regional hauling enabled
- Fleet: ~1600 haulers (10 per system with stations)
