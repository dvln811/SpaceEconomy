Fitting Mechanics

Every ship has module slots constrained by CPU and Power Grid. Choosing what to fit defines your ship's role.

Slot Types

SlotColorFitsResource Bias
High SlotRedWeapons, mining lasers, salvagers, tractor beams, remote repair, cloaks, energy warfarePG-heavy
Mid SlotYellowShield mods, propulsion (AB/MWD), EWAR (ECM, damps, painters), tackle (webs, scrams), cap boosters, scannersCPU-heavy
Low SlotGreenArmor plates/repairers/hardeners, damage mods, engineering (co-processors, reactor controls), cargo expanders, navigation passivesMixed

Hardpoints

Within High Slots, Turret Hardpoints limit how many turret weapons (lasers, railguns, autocannons, blasters) can be fitted. Launcher Hardpoints limit missile/torpedo launchers. Remaining highs can fit utility modules.

Example: A Cruiser with 5 High Slots, 4 Turret HP, 3 Launcher HP can fit:
- 4 turrets + 1 utility
- 3 launchers + 2 turrets
- 2 turrets + 2 launchers + 1 utility
But never more than 4 turrets or 3 launchers.

Stacking Penalties

Fitting multiple modules of the same effect type gives diminishing returns:

effectiveness(n) = 0.5 ^ ((n-1) / 2.22)^2
#EffectivenessExample: +10% damage mod
1st100%+10.0%
2nd87%+8.7%
3rd57%+5.7%
4th28%+2.8%
5th10%+1.0%
Applies to: damage mods, hardeners, propulsion mods, sensor boosters, tracking enhancers
Does NOT apply to: armor plates (flat HP), shield extenders (flat HP), active repairers, weapons

Tank Archetypes

Shield Tank: Mid slots. No mass penalty. Increases signature radius (easier to hit). Best against EM/Thermal.

Armor Tank: Low slots. Adds mass (slower, worse agility). Keeps signature small. Best against Kinetic/Explosive.

Hull Tank: Low slots. Cheapest option. No resists. Last resort or bait fits.
