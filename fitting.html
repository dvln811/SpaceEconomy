Ship Fitting & Stats

Every ship has a set of base stats and module slots. Fitting modules consumes CPU and Power Grid. Choosing what to fit is the core of ship customization.

Base Ship Stats

StatDescriptionAffected By

Capacitor (Cap)Energy pool that recharges over time. Active modules drain cap when used.Ship hull, cap batteries, cap rechargers

Module Slots

Ships have 5 slot types. Each module fits into exactly one slot type. Larger ships have more slots.

Slot TypeColorModules That Fit
High SlotRedWeapons (lasers, railguns, missiles, autocannons), mining lasers, tractor beams, remote repair
Mid SlotYellowShield modules, ECM, sensor boosters, propulsion (afterburner, maneuvering rig), scanners, warp stabilizers
Low SlotGreenArmor modules (plates, repairers), cargo expanders, hull reinforcements, damage upgrades, CPU/PG upgrades
Engine SlotPurpleMain engine, jump drive (one engine required, optional jump drive)
Rig SlotCyanPermanent modifications. Boost one stat at cost of another. Cannot be removed once installed.

Drone Bay

Separate from module slots. Ships with drone capability have a drone bay (measured in m3). Each drone is 5 m3. A ship with 25 m3 drone bay can deploy 5 drones simultaneously.

Fitting Rules

CPU/PG Budget: Every module has a CPU and PG requirement. Total fitted modules cannot exceed ship's CPU or PG capacity. This forces trade-offs: fit a big shield OR a big weapon, not both on a small ship.

Slot Restrictions: A weapon only fits in a High Slot. You cannot mount a railgun in a Low Slot. Engine Slots require at least one engine module (ship cannot move without one).

Rigs are Permanent: Once installed, rigs cannot be removed (only destroyed). They provide a bonus at a penalty. Example: "Mining Yield Rig: +15% mining output, -10% shield HP."

Stacking Penalties: Fitting multiple of the same module type has diminishing returns. First module = 100%, second = 87%, third = 57%, fourth = 28%.

Mass Matters: Armor plates and cargo expanders add mass, reducing agility and increasing warp fuel cost. Shield modules don't add mass but increase signature radius (easier to hit).

Design Notes

Progression Path:
- New player: Pinto Runner (fast, tiny cargo, cheap to lose)
- Early hauler: Mule Freighter (400 m3 cargo, defenseless)
- Established: Bison Mk.III (500 m3, some defense, versatile)
- Late game: Clydesdale (1500 m3, slow, needs escort in low-sec)

Tank Archetypes:
- Shield tank: Mid slots, no mass penalty, bigger sig radius
- Armor tank: Low slots, adds mass (slower), smaller sig radius
- Hull tank: Cheap, last resort (hull reinforcements in Low)

Future: Named/Faction Variants:
Standard Mk.I, Advanced Mk.II (better stats, rarer inputs), Prototype Mk.III (exotic materials, best stats). Faction variants with unique bonuses from corp loyalty.
