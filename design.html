Core Vision

"I'm navigating a complex, living system that I can never fully master."

A browser-based space economy simulation. Single-player. You are one actor, a hauler and trader, in a functioning economy driven by autonomous NPC agents. The economy is the game: emergent, agent-based, and realistic. Knowing how it works does not trivialize it, because the system is complex enough that you are always making educated bets, never guaranteed plays.

Design Pillars
  • Economy simulation IS the game - agent-based, emergent, realistic supply/demand
  • High-level decisions, not unit-level commands - you decide where to go and what to haul, not how to dodge bullets
  • Atmospheric "space trucker" vibe - Alien-era industrial space, working-class, not Star Trek polish
  • Imperfect information - you see prices where you have been recently, not omniscient market data
  • Emergent complexity - simple rules produce unpredictable outcomes

The Player Fantasy

You are a scrappy independent operator making your way through a universe of shifting economics, faction politics, and calculated risk. You read the market, pick your routes, weigh danger against profit, and try to come out ahead. The universe does not revolve around you. It is indifferent to you. You succeed by being smart, not powerful.

Inspirations

  • Eve Online - economy depth, market dynamics, information as power
  • X4 Foundations - trading loops, but less tedious micromanagement
  • Stellaris - grand scale, faction politics
  • Alien (1979) - aesthetic, working-class space, industrial grime
  • Euro Truck Simulator - the satisfaction of hauling, route planning

Core Loop

Moment-to-Moment Gameplay
  1. Read the market - check prices, identify opportunities, assess risk
  2. Plan your route - buy low here, sell high there, factor in distance, fuel, danger
  3. Execute the haul - travel through space, encounter events, make in-the-moment decisions
  4. Sell and assess - did prices shift while you were in transit? Did you profit?
  5. Reinvest - upgrade ship, expand range, take on bigger/riskier contracts

The Living Economy

How It Works

Each star system has production rates and consumption rates per commodity. Prices follow supply/demand curves. NPC agents (haulers, miners, traders) operate autonomously, creating the baseline market activity that you participate in.

Price Formula

price = base_price * (demand / supply) ^ elasticity

Key Properties

  • Emergent - prices are not scripted, they result from agent behavior
  • Cascading - a disruption at one node ripples through supply chains
  • Imperfect information - price data goes stale; you only know what you have seen recently
  • Player impact - large trades move prices; you can manipulate small markets
  • NPC adaptation - NPC traders adjust routes based on profitability, creating competition

Conflict and Opposition

This is not a combat game. You do not fight. But the universe is dangerous, and conflict shapes the economy you operate in.

Sources of Conflict

  • Faction warfare - NPC factions fight over territory. Borders shift. Safe systems become warzones. Supply chains break overnight.
  • Blockades and interdiction - you don't fight pirates, you avoid them, pay them, or find alternate routes. Risk is a cost you price into decisions.
  • Choke points - certain jump routes are the only path between regions. Control of these means control of trade flow.
  • Economic warfare - factions embargo each other. Smuggling becomes viable. Prices spike in blockaded systems.
  • Piracy - not combat encounters, but economic hazards. Lose cargo, lose time, lose reputation with insurers.

Mystery and Intrigue

  • Anomalous signals, derelict ships, uncharted systems that appear temporarily
  • Rare commodities with unknown or shifting sources
  • Faction espionage: shady contracts, smuggling jobs, intel runs
  • Events that reward paying attention to the simulation's state
  • Things that cannot be wiki'd because they are emergent, not scripted

Time Model

Real-time, always-on. The economy runs 24/7/365 on the server. The world is persistent and keeps running whether you are online or not. When you log in, you see what happened while you were away. NPC agents continued trading, prices shifted, factions moved. You operate in a living universe, not a paused one.

Technical Direction

Architecture
  • Backend: Python (Flask + asyncio), always-on server on fly.io
  • Frontend: Vanilla JS + Three.js (3D star map) + HTML/CSS panels (UI)
  • Economy Engine: Real-time agent-based tick simulation (Python, background thread)
  • Database: SQLite on persistent volume (fly.io)
  • Client-Server: WebSocket for live updates, REST for queries
  • Deployment: fly.io (shared-cpu-2x, 1GB), auto-deploy via GitHub Actions
  • Future: Free MMORPG, multiple players in shared persistent world

Build Phases

Phase 1: Living Economy - COMPLETE

  • 48-system universe (3 clusters, chokepoints, ore distribution rules)
  • 38 commodities across 5 tiers with recipe-driven production
  • Price engine: price = base * (demand/supply)^elasticity
  • 70 NPC agents (50 traders, 20 miners) with safety-aware pathfinding
  • SQLite persistence, auto-deploy via GitHub Actions
  • Two-layer navigation (inter-system + intra-system)
  • Comprehensive debug dashboard for monitoring

Phase 2: Player Can Trade (NEXT)

  • Player can buy/sell commodities at current station
  • Player can set destination, travel takes real time (ticks)
  • Fuel consumption during travel
  • Balance and cargo tracking
  • Ship upgrades (cargo capacity, fuel efficiency, speed)
  • Player mining (mining laser hardpoint on asteroid fields)

Phase 3: Blueprints and Fittings

  • Blueprint system (recipes as discoverable/tradeable items)
  • Weapons and ship fittings as equippable items
  • Ship loadout system (hardpoints, slots)
  • Damage/wear creates ongoing demand

Phase 4: Information and Risk

  • Price data staleness (you only see prices from your last visit)
  • Security zones with piracy risk (random cargo loss in low-sec)
  • Basic events (supply disruptions, price spikes, faction skirmishes)
  • Contracts and reputation

Phase 5: Factions and Depth

  • Faction territories, diplomacy, and warfare
  • Shifting borders that reshape trade routes
  • Smuggling mechanics in embargoed systems
  • Fleet ownership and infrastructure (late-game)
  • Multiplayer (shared persistent world)
