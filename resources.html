T1: Harvestable Resources

All raw materials are extracted from asteroid fields by mining vessels. Each asteroid field has a type that determines which ores can be harvested. Richer fields exist in more dangerous space.

Harvesting Mechanics

Mining Yield Formula: units_per_cycle = mining_power * field_density * ore_modifier

Cycle Time: Base 5 seconds per mining cycle. Reduced by mining laser tier.

Volume: Raw ores are bulky. A mining ship with 200 m3 cargo can carry 20 iron ore (10 m3 each) or 200 void shards (1 m3 each). This naturally limits how much cheap ore floods the market per trip while making rare materials worth dedicated runs.

Design Notes

Security Distribution:
- High-sec: Common ores only. Safe, abundant, low value per trip.
- Med-sec: Mid-grade ores + some common. Moderate risk, better margins.
- Low-sec: Rare crystals + precious metals. High risk, high reward.
- Null-sec: Exotics. Extreme danger, extreme value. Requires escort or stealth.

Economic Role: Raw ores are the foundation. They're cheap individually but consumed in enormous quantities by refineries. The volume mechanic means hauling 1000 iron ore requires multiple trips even with large cargo ships, creating natural logistics demand.
