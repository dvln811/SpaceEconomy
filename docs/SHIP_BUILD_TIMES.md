|-----------|-----------|-------|

| Frigate | 15-30 minutes | Light combat, scouts |
| Destroyer | 2-4 hours | Anti-frigate, escort |
| Cruiser | 8-16 hours | Backbone of fleets |
| Battlecruiser | 1-2 days | Heavy combat, fleet command |
| Battleship | 7-10 days | Capital-class firepower |
| Carrier | 15-20 days | Fleet support, fighter deployment |
| Dreadnought | 28 days | Siege weapons, station assault |

## Design Intent

- Small/starter ships: minutes. Players and NPCs can replace losses quickly.
- Mid-tier: hours. Meaningful but not painful to lose.
- Large civilian: days. Represents real investment, but not as long as military equivalents.
- Capital military: weeks. Strategic assets. Losing a dreadnought is a major setback that takes a month to replace.
- This creates the demand pressure: warfare destroys ships that take days/weeks to rebuild, pulling materials through the entire production chain.

## Relationship to Economy

Build time here refers to the ASSEMBLY time at a shipyard once all materials are gathered. The total time from raw ore to finished ship is much longer due to:
1. Mining the ore (continuous)
2. Refining to materials (seconds per batch)
3. Manufacturing components (minutes per batch)
4. Assembling the ship (the times listed above)

A dreadnought's 28-day build time means the shipyard is occupied for 28 days. This limits how fast factions can rebuild fleets and creates strategic value in having multiple shipyards.
